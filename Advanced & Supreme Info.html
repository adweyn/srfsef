32% + 16% * 3 = 80%		New Protection Formula

Each protection (even vanilla) gives certain 'protection points' which is multiplied by 4 to get
the damage reduction percent. So by simply dividing the damage reduction percentage of
an enchantment, we can deduce its protection points. For example, Protection IV which grants
grants 16% damage reduction has 16/4 = 4 protection points given.

Now with that out of that way, once the protection points becomes greater than
20, a new custom formula is injected in order to make use of them as vanilla
simply hard caps the damage reduction to 80%.

After the initial 20 protection points, the surplus points give 1% damage reduction instead.
However, the surplus points have a cap of 20% and stacks multiplicatively with
the 80% damage reduction. After reaching the new cap, the process repeats again, ensuring
that the full 100% damage reduction is never reached in theory.

Example

Having Advanced Protection IV in all of a mob's armor has 12*4=48 protection points.
Since 48 protection points is over the first 20, the first 20 points are used for the 80%,
which leaves us 28 protection points. 28 protection points is still over 20 protection points
so we reduce it by 20 and give a multiplicative reduction of 20%.
Now that we have 8 protection points left, it gives 8% multiplicative reduction.
In total, we have (1-0.8) * (1-0.2) * (1-0.08) = 85.28% damage reduction.
